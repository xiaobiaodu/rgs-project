RGS: Reflection-aware Gaussian Splatting via Learning Geometry Continuity for Reflective Objects


Xiaobiao Du123 · Yida Wang3 · Cheng Bi3 · Xin Yu2

1The University of Technology Sydney · 2The University of Queensland · 3Li Auto Inc.

Abstract

Volume rendering techniques, \textit{e.g.} NeRF and Gaussian Splatting, have significantly improved the quality of novel view synthesis. However, they struggle to satisfy precise rendering and geometric reconstruction in glossy regions because of the ambiguity of cross-view specular. To mitigate the specular ambiguity, we propose a reflection-aware Gaussian Splatting method, named RGS for high-quality novel view synthesis, which can model non-trivial specular without surface geometry collapse. By regularizing the geometric continuity in specular regions, the environment light can be blended precisely on glossy surfaces in novel views.

Particularly, our proposed RGS is composed of the following three contributions: 1) To avoid collapsed surfaces in reflective regions, we first regularize the rendered normal of regions with strong specular by enforcing the total variation loss and material characteristics, on top of our parameterized Gaussian model with reflective strength. 2) Apart from normal regularization, we regularize the rendered depth of 3D Gaussians with an elaborated stereo depth estimation network, further enhancing geometric continuity. 3) To further capture specular from indirect light, we propose a densification strategy guided by the reflective strength to supplement more Gaussian kernels in the reflective regions. Extensive experiments demonstrate our method consistently delivers 1dB more PSNR improvement over previous methods, achieving state-of-the-art performance.
